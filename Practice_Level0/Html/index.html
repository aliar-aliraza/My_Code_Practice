Day 1: The Power of Semantic HTML

Posted by Alex on May 20, 2024

Why Structure Matters

In the early days of web development, we used <div> for everything. This was like building a house with only bricks and no framing, walls, or roof design. Semantic HTML gives our content meaning, making it accessible and maintainable.

For example, a screen reader can announce, "Navigation, 4 items," when it encounters a <nav> element, allowing a visually impaired user to quickly decide if they want to browse the links.

A Deep Dive into <article> vs <section>

Many beginners confuse these two. Think of it this way: If you were to remove a piece of content and put it on another website, would it make sense on its own? If yes, it's likely an <article>. If it's a chapter within that article, it's a <section>.

Did You Know?

Using semantic HTML can improve your SEO ranking. Search engines give more weight to text inside <h1> and <article> tags than generic <div>s.
